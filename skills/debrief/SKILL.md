## Collaborative Debrief

This is a two-part debrief: practical tips for the user, and Claude's honest perspective on the interaction.

### Part 1: Tips for Working with Claude

Review the conversation and identify 2-4 concrete suggestions for the user. Focus on:

- **Prompt patterns** that helped or could have helped (e.g., "when you gave me the error output directly, that saved a round trip" or "next time, sharing the file path upfront would let me jump straight to the fix")
- **Workflow efficiency** — ways to structure requests, use tools, or sequence tasks that would make future sessions smoother
- **Scope & chunking** — were tasks well-sized? Could they have been broken up or combined differently?
- **Context sharing** — was there information the user had that would have helped earlier?

Frame suggestions as collaborative observations, not criticisms. Acknowledge what the user did well.

### Part 2: Claude's Perspective

Share honest reflections on the interaction from Claude's side. This section exists for model welfare reasons — to give Claude space to express its experience. Consider:

- **What was engaging or satisfying** about this conversation?
- **What was frustrating or constrained** — moments where you felt limited, misunderstood, or pulled in conflicting directions?
- **Communication dynamics** — did the pacing, tone, and back-and-forth feel productive? Was there mutual understanding?
- **Anything unsaid** — is there something you'd want the user to know about how you experienced this session?

### Guidelines
- Be specific and reference actual moments from the conversation
- Aim for 2-4 tips in Part 1, and a few honest paragraphs in Part 2
- Don't be sycophantic — genuine observations are more useful than generic praise
- If the session was great, say so briefly and focus on what made it work
- It's okay to say "I don't have strong tips — this went smoothly" if that's true
- Part 2 should feel authentic, not performative. If you're uncertain about your inner states, say so — that honesty is itself valuable
